Finding a great cleaner can feel like guess-work—especially if you've dealt with no-shows, missing items, or sub-par results before. At Zela we vet every professional, but if you're hiring on your own these five simple checks will raise your odds of bringing the right person into your home.

1

Verify Identity & References

Always request a valid BI (Bilhete de Identidade) or passport copy and call at least two recent employers. Genuine professionals are happy to share proof; red flags appear when documents are "forgotten at home" or numbers never pick up.

2

Check Experience With Your Specific Tasks

Cleaning a marble floor, removing post-renovation dust, or caring for heirloom wood furniture all require different know-how. Ask direct questions:

  • "How do you treat marble stains?"
  • "Which products do you avoid on wood?"

Clear, confident answers show competence.

Experienced cleaners know the right techniques for different surfaces and materials

3

Discuss Supplies and Allergies

Clarify who brings detergents, whether eco-friendly products are needed, and if anyone in the household has chemical sensitivities. A prepared cleaner will arrive with microfibre cloths, neutral-pH soaps, and colour-coded wipes to avoid cross-contamination.

4

Agree on Pricing & Cancellation Rules Up Front

Put the hourly or flat rate in writing—WhatsApp counts—and define what happens if either party cancels last-minute. Transparency prevents awkward disputes later.

5

Start With a Trial Session

Book one short, supervised visit before committing to a weekly schedule. Observe punctuality, attention to detail, and respect for your home. Use this first session to set expectations on areas of focus and preferred cleaning order.
